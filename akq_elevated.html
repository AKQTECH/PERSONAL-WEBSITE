The Profile

She grew up in Minneapolis in a tight-knit community of eccentric Liberian immigrants. She writes about them often.

April Korto Quioh is a Liberian-American screenwriter, director, and producer. She's written on NBC's Brooklyn Nine-Nine and Hulu's The Other Black Girl, was a Co-Executive Producer on Netflix's Survival of the Thickest, and a Supervising Producer on Apple TV+'s Loot.

She wrote, directed, and stars in Pick Me, the short film executive produced by Lilly Wachowski currently on the festival circuit. She's developing it into a feature-length romantic comedy.

She runs Palava Productions with her sister Sameliah. She's based in Los Angeles. She's repped by UTA across film and television.
